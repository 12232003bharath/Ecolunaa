Yes, absolutely! Use a tampon or, preferably, a **menstrual cup** for worry-free swimming. The cup is often favored by athletes because it creates a reliable internal seal and can be left in place for many hours.

They are completely safe! Menstrual cups do not affect virginity and are made from medical-grade silicone. There are **smaller-sized cups** designed specifically for teenagers or those with lighter flows.

Periods are a **normal, natural part of human biology**. Awareness reduces stigma, fosters empathy, and supports gender equality. When men and boys understand periods, they are more likely to support policies like period equity and environmental initiatives.

TSS is extremely rare, but it is serious. It's primarily linked to **high-absorbency tampons** left in for too long. Menstrual cups carry a lower risk than tampons, but following hygiene instructions (washing hands, cleaning the cup regularly) is essential for all product types.

Period Poverty describes the inability to afford menstrual products, often forcing individuals to miss work, school, or use unsafe alternatives. Choosing **reusable products** is a key way to help fight period poverty both locally and globally.

Menstrual fluid, by itself, has only a mild, metallic scent due to blood. Odor usually occurs when the fluid comes into contact with air or due to bacterial growth, particularly with **disposable pads**. Menstrual cups and fresh cloth pads often reduce or eliminate noticeable odor.

Mild cramping is common. However, **severe pain** that disrupts daily life is **not normal** and should be discussed with a doctor. It can sometimes indicate conditions like endometriosis or fibroids that can be managed with treatment.
